dWeb Bill Of Rights

Learn more about the dWeb Bill Of Rights which covers the users of all Peeps platforms.

< Back To Legal
  • Posted: January 23rd, 2021
  • Updated: January 23rd, 2021

I. You will always be in control of your data, and it's programmatically impossible for any application or dWeb governance non-majority to take that control away from you. On the other hand, data can be removed in the event it is related to any of the specific forms of criminal activity that are clearly outlined in the dWeb Constitution.

II. You cannot be censored or silenced for any reason, and your right to free speech and free expression on the dWeb is an inalienable right that no dWeb entity can possibly infringe upon.

III. You control your accounts and your authentication details, regardless of the authentication system being used. No application or other dWeb entity can discriminate against accounts, which means they cannot decide which users can or cannot use their platforms.

IV. Domain names and network addresses are forever owned and controlled by the original registering user and can only be blacklisted by a majority vote via the dWeb governance, and only when the domain name or network address is involved in criminal activity, as outlined in the dWeb Constitution.

V. You have the right to vote on dWeb governance members, the removal of content or activities that are unconstitutional, and network and protocol improvement proposals.

VI. You have the right to report unconstitutional behavior, content or activities taking place anywhere on the dWeb, which can then be voted on by other community members for removal.

VII. You have the right to announce your candidacy as a dWeb governance member, as long as you have never been previously voted out by dWeb's community for unconstitutional behavior.

VIII. The dWeb is a free market and web of ideas, and you have the right to create any constitutional project you see fit. No constitutional project shall ever be removed from the dWeb, nor shall a constitutional project be blacklisted by the governance, even through a majority vote.

"My bias was always to build decentralization into the net. That way it would be hard for one group to gain control. I didn't trust large central organizations. It was just in my nature to distrust them."
- Bob Taylor
