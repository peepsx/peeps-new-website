I. Illegal Activity

Many illegal activities have been described as prohibited acts in the dWeb Constitution and are therefore prohibited within the Peeps ecosystem. We do not have the ability to ban users who partake in these kinds of activities, since we do not control user data or user accounts (see Privacy Policy), although, in this instance, the dWeb Governance does have the delegated authority to ban these users from using any dWeb-based platforms, including Peeps platforms. Users of our applications can easily report these activities to the dWeb community at-large, so that violations of the dWeb Constitution can be handled appropriately. By agreeing to these terms, you agree that Peeps is in no way liable for any claim, damages or other liability, whether in action of contract, tort, or otherwise, arising from, out of or in connection with any of our platforms, apps or related experiences, including those that derive from criminal activity that may take place on Peeps platforms.

II. Payments

Payments on Peeps platforms are peer-to-peer and Peeps is never paid directly, since Peeps itself does not provide any products or services on its platforms. Although, Peeps does take a 1% service fee on many of its platforms (see VIII. Service Fee). As an example, when making a payment for a ride on dRide, your payment is sent to the driver after the ride has been completed, or another example involving the purchase of a product on dBuy, would result in your payment being sent directly to the seller when the purchase is confirmed as delivered. Peeps in most instances holds payments in an autonomous escrow account, until services have been rendered or a product has been delivered. Autonomous systems like dRide have the ability to track rides to ensure they have reached their intended destination, while platforms like dBuy have the ability to confirm via publicly available shipping data, that packages have been delivered to a user's specified shipping address.

Peeps does not have the authority to manually override its autonomous escrow system, nor does it have any centralized controls over the system itself. While products or services may not be at the quality you expect, you must deal with retailers and service providers directly, just as you would on other platforms, to resolve any issues you face with their products and services. Once funds are released from escrow, it's up to retailers and service providers whether they refund their customers. By agreeing to these terms, you understand that we make no representation, warranty, or guarantee in respect to products or services provided on any of our platforms, whether expressed or implied, including but not limited to the warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose and noninfringement. You also agree that Peeps is in no way liable for any claim, damages or other liability, whether in an action of contract, tort or otherwise, arising from, out of or in connection with any of our platforms, apps or related experiences.

III. Account Recovery

Peeps does not store or have record of any user data (see Privacy Policy). If you lose your private keys or are unable to access your account, Peeps will be unable to help you restore access to your account. Peeps does provide an autonomous account recovery service, but if you have not enabled "dRecovery" within PeepsID, prior to losing access, then there is no way that Peeps can assist in helping you restore access to your account. By agreeing to these terms, you agree that Peeps is not responsible for restoring access to your account, and understand that we disclaim any and all responsibilities and liability, in the event you are unable to access your account or you lose the data required to access your account.

IV. Injury Or Death

Peeps platforms are largely decentralized and we do not have the ability to control the content or users on our platforms. Our platforms have to rely on dWeb's Governance to remove users who partake in illegal activities and have no control over their access to our platforms. On the other hand, users of Peeps platforms have the ability to adjust their experience in various ways, where they can avoid specific users entirely or can ensure they're only provided products and services by sellers who meet a certain review threshold. It is a possibility that users suffer some sort of injury or even death, in connection with the use of Peeps platforms. By agreeing to these terms, you agree to hold Peeps harmless and also agree that in no event shall Peeps be liable for any claim, damages or other liability, whether in an action of contract, tort or otherwise, arising from, out of or in connection with Peeps platforms or the use of or other dealings in Peeps platforms.

In some instances, where Peeps platforms are available via an operating system's application marketplace or "app store", the operator of the app store may choose to remove our platforms for any reason they see fit, and our platforms would no longer be available for download or update on their operating system(s) as a result. While Peeps platforms will always be available on decentralized venues like the dWeb, some users may depend on Peeps platforms being available and functional on specific mediums, operating systems and webs, where Peeps has no control over the availability of its platforms. In the event that Peeps platforms are no longer available on specific mediums, operating systems and webs, you agree to hold Peeps harmless, regardless of the damages that arise from the lack of availability surrounding its platforms. VI. Lost Data

In some instances, users of Peeps platforms may lose access to a device that stores the private keys related to datasets that are being broadcasted on the dWeb. In the case where keys are not backed up on another device, you would no longer be able to alter or remove the data related to the lost keys. By agreeing to these terms, you agree to hold Peeps harmless in the event you lose the ability to modify or remove your data. You also agree that in no event shall Peeps be liable for any claim, damages, or other liability, whether in an action of contract, tort or otherwise, arising from, out of or in connection with losing access to your private keys related to any Peeps platform or other related software.

VIII. Service Fee

Many Peeps platforms charge a service fee, which is typically in the form of a transaction fee. Peeps takes a 1% fee across all financial transactions that take place on its platforms, to cover blockchain storage fees, development costs and marketing costs. These service fees are non-refundable, regardless of whether or not the services or products ordered in connection with a transaction, were rendered or delivered. While a Peeps autonomous escrow system may return a payment back to its original sender in the event that a product or service fails to be delivered, a service fee will still be subtracted from the payment amount, before the system returns the payment back to the original sender. By using Peeps platforms and agreeing to these terms, you agree and understand that all service fees that derive from our platforms are indeed non-refundable and that there is no remedy or claims process for the refunding of our platform-based service fees.
