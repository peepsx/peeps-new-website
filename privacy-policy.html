Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy is apart of dWeb's S.A.I.L. (Shielding American Internet Liberties) Legal Framework and was created to ensure that user privacy on the dWeb is enhanced in order to maximize user protections and to simplify how these policies are described to users.

< Back To Legal
  • Posted: January 23rd, 2021
  • Updated: January 23rd, 2021

Your data.

You as a user are always in control of your data. dWeb-based apps like ours do not use centralized servers or a centralized database system of any kind, rather, your data is either broadcasted from your computer or stored on a blockchain. In either instance, you control your data in either location and only you can modify or delete it.

It is not possible for dWeb-based apps to control your data due to the way the dWeb is designed. Therefore, no matter the circumstance, you are always in control of your data. On the other hand, the data you submit to apps is publicly available as long as you are broadcasting it publicly, or in other cases, it is available on a blockchain's publicly available ledger. It is important that you do not publicly publish information that you wouldn't want other people to see, because once published, data becomes much harder to remove since it could end up on a medium that you do not control. For this reason, it's important that you only publish information that you wish to be public.

In other instances, some platforms that use a blockchain may encrypt data before storing it on the blockchain's ledger, so that it is not publicly digestible and only readable by your device. In either event, Peeps platforms will always describe how your data is stored and whether or not it is encrypted. Data on decentralized networks is distributed and verified amongst specific users of the network, which requires it to be public. Blockchain-based data is made public by the blockchain itself, while the distribution of "off-chain" data can be limited to a specific set of peers (i.e., two people exchanging messages in a messenger application). All Peeps platforms clearly describe how your data is published and can be viewed via the "Your Data" button on any of our platforms.

Your keys and how you're authenticated.

Under no circumstance our platforms store or know your authentication details, due to the way data is stored on the dWeb. Peeps platforms use various blockchains to authenticate users using asymmetric cryptography. A blockchain stores your username and public key and is therefore able to verify the holder of its corresponding "private key" since only the private key itself can derive the public key. Using what are called "digital signature" algorithms, the blockchain is able to verify that you are indeed the holder of the private key, that corresponds to an account's public key, without exposing your private key in the process. This also allows decentralized platforms like ours to verify your identity without any centralized oversight or verification processes.

The secure storage and safekeeping of your private keys is important. Keys should be safely stored in an "Authenticator" app like PeepsID, where they are safe from outsiders and those who should never possess or have access to your private keys. An authenticator also ensures that you can verify your identity without ever exposing or interacting with your private keys, since they are under your control within the PeepsID platform itself. All Peeps platforms are integrated with PeepsID, which means that you will never have to interact with your private key(s) and are authenticated automatically by simply verifying your biometric details already stored on your device. PeepsID handles the complexities associated with cryptographic authentication. This ensures that your keys and accounts are safe from hackers and always under your control. Peeps will never know your private keys, nor can we control any aspect of your account. Ensuring that you are always in control of your private keys, also ensures that you are always in control of your account.

Your payment data.

The dWeb is designed to only work with decentralized currencies and payment processors, like Bitcoin, Ethereum, RISE and others. Transactions and account balances are publicly available by-design, although, payments can only be executed by the private key holder of the account. Payment data and payment authorization is published and handled like other data and authentication processes previously described in this policy. It's important that you understand that all payments made on the dWeb are publicly available on various payments networks, although "privacy currencies" can be used where payment-related data is made private through randomization. Many of our platforms utilize "privacy currencies" as a form of payment, although, not all of our platforms work with these currencies.

Your IP address.

The dWeb does not hide your identity or your IP address. You could use a virtual private network (VPN) to hide your IP address, although, due to the way the dWeb works, this can harbor your ability to use our platforms or your ability to communicate with others. Your IP address can be seen publicly when accessing websites unless you use a dWeb-ready VPN (a VPN that does not negatively affect the functionality of the DWEB protocol) to hide your identity. This information is only temporarily available while accessing a website and removed immediately after. There is not a historical record of who accesses specific data sets on the dWeb, it is only a record of who is currently accessing a particular data set (i.e., a website).

Personal data.

Peeps platforms may request your personal information, such as an email address, a phone number or your name, so that we can keep you up-to-date with Peeps platform releases and updates. This information is centrally stored via our own private networks, is never published publicly and will never be offered or provided to third-parties of any kind.

Government subpoenas.

We do not honor government subpoenas in regards to user-specific data requests, considering we do not possess or store any user data. The personal information we request from users is not requested via our platforms. Instead, it is submitted via our websites or other marketing mediums and is not stored in a way where it can be correlated to a particular PeepsID or user of a Peeps platform. To add to that, user data generated by our platforms is already public information and should not require a subpoena. Instead, it can be retrieved and deciphered by law enforcement, as long as the data is not encrypted. When it comes to encrypted data, only users possess the private keys that are used to encrypt the data they publish, which means we would be unable to decipher the encrypted data, even if ordered by a court to do so. On the other hand, we will assist and educate law enforcement in the process of tracking down individuals who use our applications to partake in criminal activity.

Our role.

Unlike applications on the World Wide Web that mine your data and sell your data, our role is much different. Considering we do not control your data or possess your data, this simply isn't our business model. It's kind of hard to sell data that is already freely available to the public or data that is encrypted and only decipherable by the creator of the data itself. Since the dWeb is completely server-less and Peeps platforms do not have any infrastructure costs, our platforms take a small 1% fee via in-platform payments, which allows us to expand our reach across the globe and grow our development efforts in the process. We will never sell your data, nor could we. In fact, we generate more revenue by doing things honestly and out in the open.

Your safety and privacy matters.

dWeb's people-first digital policies and agenda were created to ensure that you always control your data, that your account is kept out of the hands of those who are not authorized to use it and that you and your family are kept as far away from criminal activity as possible. At Peeps we have happily adopted those policies, and as users of the dWeb, we have agreed to uphold the dWeb's Constitution so that your digital rights and freedoms are preserved. Your safety and your privacy will always be our top priorities and we will continue to update this policy and others to ensure you understand how privacy works within our platforms and to expand upon these protections where possible.
